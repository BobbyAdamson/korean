## Grammar points

### 더라고요 (Lesson 3)

V stem /A stem + 더라고요. - (I found/realized/felt directly that ~ was/did V/A)

The Korean grammar form -더라고요 is used to express something the speaker experienced, saw, or realized firsthand. It's a form of reported speech that conveys the speaker's reflection or observation of an event or situation. This form often implies that the speaker had some kind of new realization or discovery while witnessing the event.

#### Key points

    - Firsthand Observation: The speaker must have personally seen, heard, or experienced the situation. It's not used for hearsay or things the speaker was told by someone else.
        - Example: 날씨가 갑자기 많이 추워지더라고요 (I noticed the weather suddenly got very cold).
    - Surprise or Realization: It often carries a nuance of realization, surprise, or reflection about what was observed.
        - Example: 그 사람은 생각보다 친절하더라고요 (I found that person to be kinder than I thought).
    - Tense: Although -더라고요 describes past experiences or realizations, it’s often used in a conversational context to recount something to others in the present. It’s a past reflective marker.

#### Examples

- 친구가 새 차를 샀더라고요 (I noticed my friend bought a new car).
- 음식을 먹어 보니까 생각보다 맵더라고요 (After trying the food, I found it spicier than I thought).

#### MORE

- 을까 해요 - (be thinking of V, be planning to V) Not fixed or firmly decided/can be changed later
  - Example
    - 시간이 있으면 친구를 만날까 해요.
    - 퇴직하면 시골에서 살까 해요.
- 더라고요 - V stem /A stem + 더라고요. - (I found/realized/felt directly that ~ was/did V/A)
  - Example
    - 친구가 새 차를 샀더라고요 (I noticed my friend bought a new car).
    - 음식을 먹어 보니까 생각보다 맵더라고요 (After trying the food, I found it spicier than I thought).
- ㄹ 테니까 - supposition and suggestion/advice
  - Example
    - 내가 점심을 살 테니까 같이 가요.
    - 내일은 많이 더울 테니까 집에서 쉬는 게 어때요?!
    - 내가 요리할 테니까 너는 설거지해.
    - 비가 올 테니까 우산을 가져가세요.
- ㄹ 뻔했어요 - relief that something shocking/unfortunate ALMOST happened
  - Example
    - 배가 고파서 죽을 뻔 했어요.
- V + 다가 - I was doing something and then this other thing interuppted it, used to indicate a sudden change or transition from one event to another
  - Example
    - 밥을 먹다가 전화를 받았어요
    - 집에 가다가 친구를 만났어요
    - 공부하다가 텔레비전을 봤어요
- N1 + 에다(가) N2 +까지
  - from N1 to N2 something is happening!
  - Used to emphasize a situation is getting worse as one thing is added to another
  - Example
    - 전기세에다가 수도세까지 전부 오르네요
- V = 자미자 (곧/바로) - indicates that two events happened immediately in sequence
  - Example
    - 졸업하자마자 결혼했어요: as soon as I graduated I got married
- V 던 - 1. It is used to when recalling a past event or state of affairs that has not continued to the present.
  - Example
    - 어제 점심 때 먹었던 음식 이름이 뭐예요?
    - 지난 주에 배웠던 문법이 너무 어려워요.
    - 옛날에 좋아했던 사람이에요.

(First, as soon as I arrived in Jeju, I went to the beach. Swimming at the beach felt so refreshing and nice.)

(Jeju has many beautiful beaches like **Hyeopjae Beach**, so I’m thinking about visiting several of them.)

하지만 날씨가 갑자기 좋아질지도 모르니까 우산을 꼭 가지고 가세요.  
(However, the weather almost turned bad, so you must bring an umbrella.)

don’t forget to check the weather forecast before your trip.)

또, 제주도에 가면 딱새우와 흑돼지를 꼭 먹어 보세요. 정말 맛있더라고요.  
(Also, when you go to Jeju, make sure to try 딱새우 and 흑돼지, which I used to eat often before. They were really delicious.)

이 음식들은 제주도에서만 먹을 수 있어서 먹어 볼 만해요. 제주도는 관광지라서 가격이 좀 비쌀 테니까 예산을 잘 세우세요.

제주도는 해변, 음식, 산 등 할 것도 많고 볼 것도 많아서 정말 가볼 만한 곳이에요. 꼭 한 번 가 보세요!  
(Jeju has so much to do and see, from beaches to food and mountains, so it’s really worth visiting. You should definitely go!)
